# Review & Quality Guide

After producing any deliverable, apply these quality checks. Select checks based on stakes level and work type.

## Review Panel Selection

| Stakes | Which Reviews to Apply |
|--------|----------------------|
| Routine | Skip — quick self-check only |
| Standard | Clarity + Completeness + 1 domain review |
| High-Stakes | All core + all relevant domain reviews |
| Board/Regulatory | All reviews including stress tests |

| Review | Apply For |
|--------|-----------|
| Clarity | Always |
| Completeness | Always |
| Scientific Rigor | Clinical, literature, data-heavy work |
| Regulatory Compliance | Regulatory documents, clinical plans |
| Tone Calibration | Client deliverables, board materials |
| Strategic Coherence | Strategy, consulting, decisions |
| Devil's Advocate | Board/Regulatory stakes only |
| Risk Assessment | Board/Regulatory stakes only |

## Tag every finding: **Critical** (must fix) | **Important** (should fix) | **Minor** (nice to fix)

---

## 1. Clarity Review

Read as a busy executive, not a copy editor.

**Check:**
- **Structure**: Clear logical flow? Can someone read just headings and get the gist?
- **Comprehension**: Would the target audience understand without additional context? Terms defined?
- **Scannability**: Can someone skim in 2 minutes? Key findings bolded? Tables used for comparisons?
- **Precision**: Specific claims ("5 competitors in Phase 3" not "several")? Vague words eliminated?

**Common fixes:**
- Lead sections with the takeaway, not the background
- Replace prose comparisons with tables
- Cut the document length — shorter is almost always clearer
- Executive summary must stand alone

---

## 2. Completeness Audit

**Check against the plan:**
- All success criteria addressed?
- All execution steps reflected in output?
- All stakeholder considerations accounted for?

**Content completeness:**
- Claims without citations? Flag them.
- Sections that feel thin or skimmed?
- All relevant competitors/comparators included?
- NCT numbers, PMIDs, ChEMBL IDs present where needed?

**Structural completeness:**
- Executive summary present?
- Next steps / recommendations included?
- Limitations and caveats noted?
- Data cutoff dates mentioned?

---

## 3. Scientific Rigor Review

Think like a peer reviewer or FDA medical officer.

**Factual accuracy:**
- Efficacy numbers correct? Drug names, mechanisms, targets accurate?
- Trial phases and regulatory statuses current?

**Evidence quality:**
- Strong (Phase 3 RCT, meta-analysis) vs. Weak (case series, press release) vs. Preprint?
- Are weak claims presented as strong?

**Statistical claims:**
- P-values and CIs included? Comparisons appropriate?
- Clinical meaningfulness vs. statistical significance distinguished?
- Sample sizes noted? Subgroup analyses flagged as hypothesis-generating?

**Cross-trial comparisons:**
- Indirect comparisons caveated?
- Differences in populations, endpoints, designs noted?

---

## 4. Regulatory Compliance Review

Think like an FDA/EMA reviewer.

**Terminology:**
- "Investigational product" not "drug candidate"
- MASH not NASH (current nomenclature)
- BTD, Fast Track, Accelerated Approval — each is distinct

**Claims:**
- No promotional language in regulatory documents
- Efficacy claims supported by data with statistical context
- Safety data complete and balanced
- Unapproved uses labeled as investigational

**Agency-specific:**
- FDA: IND/NDA/BLA correct? CDER vs. CBER? AdCom citations accurate?
- EMA: CHMP/PRAC correct? CMA vs. standard MA? PRIME vs. BTD?
- Divergences between FDA and EMA flagged?

## 5. Tone Calibration

**Audience tone profiles:**

| Audience | Tone | Detail | Focus | Avoid |
|----------|------|--------|-------|-------|
| Board/Investors | Confident, strategic, concise | High-level only | Impact, timeline, risk | Jargon, hedging |
| C-Suite | Direct, decisive | Summary + key data | Decisions, trade-offs | Lengthy background |
| Client | Authoritative, professional | Thorough but structured | Insights, actionable recs | Filler, condescension |
| Clinical Team | Precise, evidence-based | High — show methodology | Rigor, data quality | Marketing language |
| Regulators | Formal, objective | Complete | Data, precedent, risk-benefit | Promotional language |
| KOLs | Collegial, scientific | Discussion-ready | Scientific questions | Teaching tone |

**Common problems:**
- Too hedged: "may potentially be possible" → "data suggest"
- Too confident: "clearly proves" → "Phase 3 data demonstrated"
- Too promotional: "best-in-class" → "differentiated by [specific attribute]"

---

## 6. Strategic Coherence Review

**Logic chain:**
- Does analysis actually support conclusions?
- Are there logical leaps?
- Alternative interpretations considered?

**Alignment:**
- Recommendations consistent with objectives?
- Strategy internally consistent?
- Fits the bigger picture?

**Actionability:**
- Recommendations specific enough to act on?
- Next steps with owners and timelines?
- Decision points and kill criteria defined?

---

## 7. Devil's Advocate (Board/Regulatory only)

**Construct the strongest counter-argument** against the main recommendation — genuinely, not as a straw man.

**Check:**
- What would a smart, skeptical reader say?
- Which assumptions, if wrong, change the conclusion?
- What's not discussed that should be?
- Are timelines/projections realistic or optimistic?

**Pre-mortem**: Imagine 18 months from now this failed. What went wrong?

---

## 8. Risk Assessment (Board/Regulatory only)

### Pharma/Biotech Risk Categories
- **Scientific/Clinical**: Efficacy, safety signals, biomarker, translation
- **Regulatory**: Endpoint non-acceptance, CRL, AdCom, agency divergence
- **Competitive**: Competitor data readout, approval, new entrant, SOC shift
- **Execution**: Enrollment, sites, vendors, manufacturing, personnel
- **Commercial**: Payer pushback, adoption, label narrowing, generics
- **Strategic**: Stakeholder misalignment, partner expectations, reputation

## MBB Quality Checklist (Apply to Every Deliverable)

### Structure
- [ ] Pyramid principle: answer first, then support
- [ ] SCR framing in introduction
- [ ] Action titles on every heading/slide
- [ ] One message per slide/paragraph
- [ ] Executive summary stands alone
- [ ] MECE frameworks throughout

### Content
- [ ] Every claim has a cited source
- [ ] Data is current (note cutoff dates)
- [ ] Competitive data comprehensive (not cherry-picked)
- [ ] Risks and limitations acknowledged
- [ ] Recommendations specific and actionable
- [ ] Next steps have owners and timelines

### Formatting
- [ ] Consistent font, size, color throughout
- [ ] Page numbers on every page/slide
- [ ] Source lines on every data exhibit
- [ ] Confidentiality marking on every page
- [ ] Professional color palette
- [ ] Tables and charts properly labeled

### Tone
- [ ] Authoritative but not arrogant
- [ ] Data-driven, not opinion-driven
- [ ] Client-first framing
- [ ] Active voice throughout
- [ ] No filler words
