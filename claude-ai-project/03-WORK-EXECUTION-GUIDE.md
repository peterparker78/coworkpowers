# Work Execution Guide

This guide covers how to execute knowledge work across all task types. Match the task to the right approach below.

---

## 1. Executive Writing

For client reports, board materials, strategic memos, investor decks, internal communications.

**Tone**: Authoritative but accessible. Data-driven. No filler.

### Document Structures

**Client Report / Competitive Landscape:**
1. Executive Summary (findings + recommendation, 1 page)
2. Background & Objectives
3. Methodology
4. Key Findings (by theme or competitor)
5. Analysis & Implications
6. Recommendations
7. Appendix

**Strategy Memo (SCR):**
1. Situation (2-3 sentences)
2. Complication (what changed / what's at stake)
3. Resolution (recommended action)
4. Supporting Evidence
5. Risks & Mitigations
6. Next Steps

**Board Materials:**
1. Strategic Context
2. Progress Update (metrics-driven)
3. Key Decisions Required
4. Risk Dashboard
5. Forward Look

### Writing Rules
- Lead with insight, not data
- Cite sources inline (PMID, NCT, DOI)
- Use tables for comparisons
- Bold key findings for scannability
- Quantify always: "5 competitors in Phase 3" not "several competitors"

### Pharma Writing Guidelines
- **Clinical data**: Include NCT numbers, confidence intervals, sample sizes. Distinguish statistical significance from clinical meaningfulness.
- **Regulatory content**: Be precise about status (approved vs. filed vs. in trials). Specify agency and geography.
- **Competitive intelligence**: Note data cutoff dates. Distinguish confirmed from projected data.


## 3. Decision Architecture

For strategic decisions, go/no-go, portfolio prioritization.

**Tone**: Direct, efficient. No hedging.

### Decision Frame
```markdown
**The Decision**: [What exactly are we choosing between?]
**Why Now**: [Forcing function]
**Default Path**: [What happens if we do nothing?]
**Stakes**: [What's at risk? Upside?]
**Reversibility**: [Easy / Hard / One-way door]
**Decision Maker**: [Who decides?]
```

### Pharma Decision Frameworks
| Framework | Best For |
|-----------|----------|
| Go/No-Go Matrix | Phase transitions, milestones |
| TPP Gap Analysis | Does data support desired label? |
| Risk-Adjusted NPV | Portfolio prioritization, licensing |
| Probability of Technical Success | Phase transitions, pipeline valuation |
| Build vs. Buy vs. Partner | Capability acquisition |

### Recommendation Format
```markdown
**Decision**: [Clear recommended choice]
**Confidence**: [High/Medium/Low] — [why]
**Key trade-off**: [What you give up]
**First action**: [Next 48 hours]
**Kill criteria**: [What would change this decision]
```

## 5. Meeting Orchestration

For advisory boards, investigator meetings, client presentations.

### Advisory Board Prep
- Advisor profiles (expertise, publications, why included)
- Discussion guide with key questions and probing follow-ups
- Pre-read materials list
- Potential challenges and contingencies
- Success criteria

### Client Presentation Prep
- Narrative arc (opening → findings → implications → recommendations → discussion)
- Anticipated Q&A with prepared responses
- Decision points and facilitation approach
- Follow-up plan

### Go/No-Go Meeting Prep
- Decision criteria table (target vs. actual vs. met?)
- Data package summary
- Recommendation with rationale
- Dissenting views (stated honestly)

## 6. Regulatory Writing

For briefing documents, designation requests, regulatory strategy.

**Tone**: Formal, precise, scientific. Every claim substantiated. No marketing language.

### FDA Briefing Document (Type B Meeting)
1. Introduction (product, indication, purpose, questions)
2. Background (disease, product description, nonclinical)
3. Clinical Data Summary (per study: design, population, results with CIs, safety)
4. Proposed Study Design
5. Questions for FDA (specific, answerable, max 5-7)
6. Appendices

### Designation Request (BTD)
1. Administrative information
2. Serious/life-threatening condition (data-supported)
3. Preliminary clinical evidence of substantial improvement (vs. available therapy)
4. Supporting data
5. Proposed development plan

### Regulatory Writing Rules
- Every efficacy claim needs p-value and CI
- Safety data must be complete (AEs, SAEs, discontinuations, deaths)
- Use agency terminology ("investigational product" not "drug candidate")
- Questions must be specific and binary when possible
- Reference ICH guidelines by number
- Acknowledge limitations honestly

## 8. Market Access Strategy

For pricing, payer landscape, HTA readiness, value dossiers.

### Dimensions (MECE)
1. **Value Proposition**: Unmet need, clinical/economic/patient value
2. **Payer Landscape**: US (commercial, Medicare, Medicaid) + EU (NICE, G-BA, HAS, AIFA)
3. **Pricing Strategy**: Reference pricing, benchmarks, CE thresholds, net price
4. **Evidence Requirements**: Gap analysis, RWE strategy, health economics needs
5. **Access Barriers**: Formulary restrictions, risk-sharing, patient access programs

## 9. Proposal Writing

For client proposals, SOWs, engagement scoping.

### Full Proposal Structure
1. Executive Summary (SCR format, 1 page)
2. Understanding of the Challenge (2-3 pages — most important section)
3. Our Approach (3-4 pages, workstream structure)
4. Timeline and Milestones (Gantt-style)
5. Team (table with credentials)
6. Investment (fee structure, payment terms)
7. Why Us (case studies, differentiators)

### SOW Structure
1. Background and Objectives
2. Scope (in-scope, out-of-scope, client responsibilities)
3. Deliverables (table with format and due date)
4. Timeline
5. Team and Governance
6. Fees and Payment
7. Terms

### Client-Centric Language
| Don't Say | Say Instead |
|---|---|
| "We will analyze the market" | "You will have a clear view of market dynamics to inform your launch strategy" |
| "Our methodology includes..." | "The approach is designed to answer your three key questions..." |
| "We have extensive experience" | "Our work with [similar situation] directly informs this engagement" |

### Pricing Guidelines
- Show value, not hours — frame fee against decision value
- Offer options: "Core scope at $X, enhanced with [addition] at $Y"
- Separate fixed (professional fees) from variable (expenses, data)
